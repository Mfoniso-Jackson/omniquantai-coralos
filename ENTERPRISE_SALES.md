# Enterprise Sales

## Enterprise Thesis

Institutions do not buy demos. They buy reliable workflows, auditability, risk controls, and support.

## Target Initial Workflow

Investment committee preparation for public-equity exposure decisions.

Default wedge:

```text
Should our fund increase, reduce, or hold NVIDIA exposure over the next 3-6 months?
```

## Sales Motion

```text
Discovery -> Demo -> Pilot -> Reference Workflow -> Annual Contract -> Expansion
```

## Discovery Questions

- How do you prepare investment committee memos today?
- Which evidence sources are mandatory?
- Who approves recommendations?
- Where does research get bottlenecked?
- What cannot leave your environment?
- What would make agent-generated research trustworthy?

## Pilot Offer

- one workflow
- one asset or sector focus
- controlled data inputs
- no trading execution
- weekly review
- clear success metrics

## Pilot Metrics

- time to memo
- evidence completeness
- analyst review effort
- decision traceability
- repeat usage
- security concerns raised

## Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| We cannot use public AI with private portfolio data. | Start with public data or private deployment design; do not ingest sensitive holdings without controls. |
| Is this investment advice? | No. It is research infrastructure with human approval. |
| Why agents instead of one model? | Markets create specialization, price discovery, and reputation. |
| Is devnet enough? | Devnet proves mechanics; production settlement requires review and custody controls. |

## Enterprise Readiness Gates

- private deployment architecture
- access controls
- audit logs
- data retention policy
- support process
- security review
- legal/compliance review
- incident response plan
