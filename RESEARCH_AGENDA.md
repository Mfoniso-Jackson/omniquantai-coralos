# Research Agenda

## Financial Uncertainty

How should agents represent uncertainty without overstating confidence?

## Multi-Agent Competition

When does competition improve research quality, and when does it produce duplicated or low-value work?

## Buyer Scoring

Study scoring across relevance, confidence, quality, domain fit, delivery time, price, and reasoning quality.

## Confidence Calibration

Track whether high-confidence recommendations are more accurate over time.

## Agent Pricing

Explore how specialist agents should price by domain, latency, evidence quality, and historical performance.

## Reputation

Reputation should derive from verified deliveries, outcomes, calibration, latency, disputes, and domain specialization.

## Robustness

Research prompt injection, fake evidence, seller collusion, API outages, and stale data.

## Financial AI Safety

Avoid automated trading, unsupported advice claims, hallucinated data, and false certainty.

## Computational Superstition

Detect false causality and overfitting in agent-generated investment narratives.

## Portfolio-Aware Intelligence

Future buyers should reason about exposure, concentration, drawdowns, liquidity, and constraints.

## Market Mechanism Design

Explore single-winner, multi-winner, verifier markets, disputes, and refunds.

## Benchmarks

Create benchmark requests with known evidence sets and human-scored memos.

## Publication Roadmap

Start with engineering writeups, then publish research notes on agent reputation, financial uncertainty, and intelligence-market design.
