# Go-To-Market

## ICP

Initial customer profile:

- small investment teams
- boutique asset managers
- independent research shops
- family offices
- crypto funds
- quant developers

## Problem Interviews

Validate:

- how teams prepare investment committee memos
- where research work gets bottlenecked
- what evidence makes a recommendation trusted
- how teams evaluate outside research
- what they would pay for faster memo preparation

## Design Partner Offer

Current candidate offer:

- one public-equity exposure decision
- four specialist-agent perspectives
- structured Investment Committee Memo
- clear source labels and timestamps
- no trading execution
- human review required

## Pilot Metrics

- time saved
- memo usefulness
- evidence quality
- buyer trust in seller selection
- willingness to repeat
- willingness to pay

## Pricing Hypotheses

These are hypotheses, not forecasts:

- per-research-request fee
- professional subscription
- team workspace subscription
- private market setup fee
- API or SDK plan for developers

## Sales Process

1. Problem interview
2. Demo with their example decision
3. Controlled pilot
4. Review memo quality and workflow fit
5. Expand to recurring decisions

## Objections

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| Is this financial advice? | No. It is research support and requires human review. |
| Why pay agents? | Payment creates accountability and a history of completed work. |
| Why not use one model? | Competition enables specialization, pricing, and reputation. |
| Is mainnet required? | No. Devnet proves settlement mechanics; mainnet requires review. |

## Enterprise Readiness Gates

- private deployment
- access controls
- audit logs
- data retention policy
- compliance review
- security review
- support process
